The Science In Plain English

Every Company Has a Filter. ISC Measures It.

A healthy company absorbs financial shocks — when fuel prices spike or a competitor undercuts, margins dip but recover. The system self-corrects. A company in structural decline amplifies shocks — each problem feeds the next. ISC measures which way a company is going, before the financial statements fully reflect it.

Regulated (C < −0.10)

Transitioning (−0.10 to 0.30)

Bifurcating (C > 0.30)
Amplifying stress. The pre-collapse pattern.

Ryan W. Malone is an independent researcher and financial systems architect who spent over a decade building financial analytics tools for engineering and construction firms. He noticed that corporate financial collapse follows a predictable mathematical pattern — the same dynamics that appear in other complex systems under stress. Three years of validation work followed, testing the measurement across 14 different domains and 187 historical events.

Why ISC Financial Is Better

Most financial tools measure the state of a company — what the numbers say right now. ISC measures the dynamics — whether the financial system is moving toward stability or away from it. That is a fundamentally different measurement, and it shows up earlier.

The ISC coupling score C = corr(Var_W, AR1_W) measures the correlation between rolling variance and rolling autocorrelation. In healthy companies, variance spikes get damped out — C stays negative. In deteriorating companies, variance and persistence rise together — C turns positive. This measurement has AUC=0.96 on 45 historical failure cases and leads Altman Z by 1–5 quarters in deteriorating companies.
