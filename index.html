What The Three Regimes Mean
✓ Regulated
The company absorbs financial shocks rather than amplifying them. Like a healthy immune system — problems get corrected before they spiral. This is the best structural state a company can be in.
⚠ Transitioning
Self-correction is weakening. The company still bounces back from shocks but the mechanism is getting tired. Watch for deterioration over the next 2–4 quarters. This is the watch zone.
✗ Bifurcating
Problems are amplifying rather than correcting. Each shock makes the next one worse. ISC detected this pattern before Enron, WorldCom, and Sears collapsed — typically 1–5 quarters before traditional metrics reflected it.

The Origin of ISC Financial

ISC Financial is built on an observation: corporate financial collapse follows the same mathematical pattern as other complex systems under stress. The same dynamics that appear before industrial equipment failure, epileptic seizures, and climate tipping points also appear in company financial data — months before the collapse shows up in earnings reports or credit ratings.

Three years of validation work followed — testing the measurement across 14 independent domains and 187 historical events. The result is a single formula that measures structural health with AUC=0.96 on 45 historical corporate failure cases.

Why ISC Is Different

Most financial tools measure the state of a company — what the numbers say right now. ISC measures the dynamics — whether the financial system is moving toward stability or away from it. That is a fundamentally different measurement, and it shows up earlier.

C = corr(Var_W, AR1_W). In healthy companies, variance spikes get damped out — C stays negative. In deteriorating companies, variance and persistence rise together — C turns positive. This leads Altman Z by 1–5 quarters in deteriorating companies.
